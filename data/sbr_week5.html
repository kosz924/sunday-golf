The 49ers visit the Rams on Thursday Night Football, while the Bills host the Patriots on Sunday Night Football. Sunday action opens with an International Series game as the Vikings and Browns clash in London, and the week closes with the Chiefs and Jaguars on Monday Night Football.

How do NFL spread odds work?

When betting on the NFL, the favorite needs to win by a certain number of points to cover the "point spread" - also known as the betting line. Conversely, the underdog needs to lose by fewer than the points displayed in the spread or win outright to cash. For example, a 3-point favorite would need to win by more than a field goal to reward bettors.

How do NFL moneyline odds work?

When betting on the NFL, the underdog will have the "longer" odds and is typically priced at "plus-money" odds that indicate the profit on a $100 bet. For example, a team with +150 odds would turn a $100 bet into a $150 profit - or a $10 bet into a $15 profit - according to our odds converter.
